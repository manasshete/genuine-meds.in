What is Ibuprofen?

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) commonly used to reduce pain, fever, and inflammation. It works by blocking the production of certain natural substances that cause inflammation in the body. Ibuprofen is available over-the-counter and by prescription in higher doses.

Uses

Ibuprofen is commonly used to treat:

  • Headaches and migraines
  • Muscle aches and pains
  • Arthritis pain and inflammation
  • Back pain
  • Dental pain
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Fever reduction
  • Minor injuries and sprains

Dosage

For Adults (Over-the-Counter):

  • Pain/Fever: 200-400 mg every 4-6 hours as needed
  • Maximum daily dose: 1,200 mg (unless directed by doctor)
  • Do not exceed 3 days for fever or 10 days for pain without consulting a doctor

For Children (6 months to 12 years):

  • Dosage based on weight: 5-10 mg per kg of body weight
  • Given every 6-8 hours as needed
  • Always consult pediatrician for proper dosing

Prescription Strength:

  • Higher doses available by prescription for severe conditions
  • Follow doctor's instructions exactly

Side Effects

Common side effects may include:

  • Stomach upset or heartburn
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Gas or bloating
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Dizziness
  • Headache

Serious side effects (seek immediate medical attention):

  • Signs of stomach bleeding (black, bloody, or tarry stools)
  • Severe stomach pain
  • Chest pain or shortness of breath
  • Weakness on one side of the body
  • Slurred speech
  • Swelling of face, lips, tongue, or throat
  • Severe skin reactions or rash
  • Changes in urination

Precautions

Before taking ibuprofen, tell your doctor if you have:

  • History of stomach ulcers or bleeding
  • Heart disease or high blood pressure
  • Kidney or liver disease
  • Asthma
  • Blood clotting disorders
  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Lupus
  • History of stroke

Special considerations:

  • Take with food or milk to reduce stomach irritation
  • Avoid alcohol while taking ibuprofen
  • Stay hydrated, especially during exercise or hot weather
  • May increase risk of heart attack or stroke with long-term use
  • Not recommended during the last 3 months of pregnancy

Drug Interactions

Ibuprofen may interact with:

  • Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin)
  • Blood pressure medications (ACE inhibitors, diuretics)
  • Other NSAIDs
  • Corticosteroids
  • Lithium
  • Methotrexate
  • Cyclosporine
  • Certain antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs)

Always inform your healthcare provider about all medications and supplements you are taking.

In case of overdose, contact poison control immediately at 1-800-222-1222 or seek emergency medical attention. Symptoms of overdose may include severe stomach pain, vomiting blood, difficulty breathing, extreme drowsiness, or loss of consciousness.

When to Avoid Ibuprofen

Do not take ibuprofen if you:

  • Are allergic to ibuprofen or other NSAIDs
  • Have had asthma attacks or severe allergic reactions to aspirin or NSAIDs
  • Are about to have or recently had heart bypass surgery
  • Have severe kidney or liver disease
  • Are in the third trimester of pregnancy
